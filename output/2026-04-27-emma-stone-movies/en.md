# 10 Emma Stone Movies You Can Watch Right Now on Nkiri

Emma Stone has been one of the most consistent actors in Hollywood for over a decade. From teen comedies to Oscar-winning performances, her filmography covers a pretty wide range. If you're looking to binge some of her best work, a good number of emma stone movies are available to stream on [Nkiri](https://thenkiri.site).

Here's a list of her standout films — the ones worth your time, in no particular order.

## 1. The Amazing Spider-Man (2012)

Stone plays Gwen Stacy, Peter Parker's love interest in this reboot of the Spider-Man franchise. She's not the typical damsel-in-distress — Gwen is smart, funny, and holds her own in every scene she's in. The chemistry between Stone and Andrew Garfield is one of the main reasons this movie works as well as it does.

A big-budget superhero movie with an actual human relationship at its center. The sequel (*The Amazing Spider-Man 2*, 2014) is also worth a watch if you want to see where their story goes.

## 2. Poor Things (2023)

Stone's second Oscar win came from this one. She plays Bella Baxter, a woman brought back to life with a child's brain in an adult body. It sounds weird, and it is — but in the best way. Directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, the film is visually unlike anything else and Stone's performance is completely unhinged in a way that works.

Not for everyone, but if you like films that take risks, this is essential viewing.

## 3. Easy A (2010)

The movie that made Emma Stone a star. She plays Olive, a high school student who pretends to have a scandalous life and watches the rumors spiral out of control. It's sharp, funny, and Stone carries the entire film with her comedic timing.

Still holds up as one of the best teen comedies of the 2010s.

## 4. The Help (2011)

Set in 1960s Mississippi, Stone plays a young white journalist who decides to write a book from the perspective of Black maids working for white families. The film tackles heavy subject matter, and while Stone is part of an ensemble cast (Viola Davis and Octavia Spencer deliver the standout performances), she holds her own as the narrative anchor.

A solid drama that's worth watching for the performances alone.

## 5. Zombieland (2009)

A zombie comedy where Stone plays Wichita, a con artist surviving the apocalypse with her younger sister. It's not trying to be high art — it's just a fun, well-paced ride with a great cast (Jesse Eisenberg, Woody Harrelson, Abigail Breslin). Stone's character is the cool one, and she plays it without overdoing it.

Good movie for a casual night. There's also a sequel, *Zombieland: Double Tap* (2019), if you want more.

## 6. Birdman (2014)

Stone plays Sam, the recovering-addict daughter of Michael Keaton's character — a washed-up actor trying to revive his career through Broadway. It's a backstage drama shot to look like one continuous take, and Stone's performance earned her an Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actress.

Her scenes with Keaton are some of the best in the film. Raw and uncomfortable in a good way.

## 7. Crazy, Stupid, Love (2011)

A romantic comedy with a surprisingly good cast — Stone, Steve Carell, Ryan Gosling, Julianne Moore. Stone plays a law student who catches the eye of Gosling's character. Their scenes together are genuinely funny, and she manages to make a relatively small role memorable.

Light, entertaining, and doesn't overstay its welcome.

## 8. The Favourite (2018)

Another Yorgos Lanthimos collaboration. Stone plays Abigail, a scheming servant who worms her way into the favor of Queen Anne (Olivia Colman) in 18th-century England. It's a period piece but nothing like the usual costume drama — it's dark, funny, and brutally sharp.

Stone playing a manipulative character is a nice change of pace from her usual likable roles.

## 9. The Amazing Spider-Man 2 (2014)

The sequel picks up where the first film left off. Peter and Gwen's relationship gets more complicated, and the stakes are higher. Stone has more to do in this one — Gwen isn't just a supporting character here, she drives part of the plot herself.

The ending is one of the most memorable in any superhero movie. If you watched the first one on Nkiri, the sequel is right there too.

## 10. The Croods (2013)

An animated film where Stone voices Eep, a teenage cavegirl who wants to explore the world beyond her family's cave. It's a family movie but genuinely funny — Ryan Reynolds and Nicolas Cage round out the voice cast. The sequel, *The Croods: A New Age* (2020), is also worth a watch.

A lighter option if you want something you can put on without thinking too hard.
