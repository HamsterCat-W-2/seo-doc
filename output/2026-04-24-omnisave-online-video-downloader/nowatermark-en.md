## Where's the Watermark Actually From?

There are really two scenarios.

The first one is the downloader adding it. A lot of free download sites re-encode your video and slap their logo on it as a "thanks for using our free service" move. Switching to a different tool fixes this. Omnisave Online Video Downloader doesn't do that — it gives you the raw video file without overlaying anything.

The second scenario is the platform's own watermark. If you download a video from TikTok or Douyin, there's going to be a username and platform logo on it no matter what tool you use. That watermark was rendered into the video when it was uploaded. It's part of the image now. No downloader can strip it out — the only options are cropping or blurring, and neither looks great.

So the real question isn't "which downloader removes watermarks" — it's "does this video have a watermark to begin with?"
